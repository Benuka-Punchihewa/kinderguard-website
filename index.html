Project Scope

Literature Survey

Source: https://www.sundaytimes.lk

Child sexual abuse has been established to have enduring and often lifelong consequences on the mental health of victims. Its ramifications extend to physical health, mental well-being, education, employment opportunities, and overall quality of life for the affected children. It is disconcerting to note that approximately 91% of reported incidents involve perpetrators known to the victim. Alarming trends reveal that children often refrain from disclosing abusive incidents to caregivers or authorities due to fear or shame. Furthermore, some victims lack the maturity to identify the abuse until they reach adulthood.

The definition of child abuse encompasses individuals under the age of 18 engaged in any form of non-consensual or non-knowledgeable sexual activity. Moreover, any sexual activity occurring prior to reaching the age of consent is classified as child abuse. This maltreatment can manifest in both physical and non-physical forms.

Child sexual abuse casts a multitude of negative ramifications upon a child's life, spanning psychological, behavioral, interpersonal, and physical spheres. Among these, the psychological toll is profound, encompassing a spectrum of effects that can be immediate or manifest later in life. Delayed psychological effects often stem from sexual abuse occurring without the child's awareness

The compounding effects of these issues can exacerbate vulnerability to conditions such as HIV, teen pregnancy, and sexually transmitted infections (STIs). These health challenges are closely linked to the behavioral issues stemming from the traumatic experience of child sexual abuse. The intricate web of interactions between psychological, behavioral, interpersonal, and physical domains underscores the profound and enduring nature of the consequences faced by survivors. Consequently, addressing the holistic well-being of individuals affected by child sexual abuse requires a comprehensive and multidisciplinary approach.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps were found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & Classification

There are currently no IoT-based solutions that leverage AI to detect child abuse. Utilizing technologies like speech analysis, motion detection, and geofencing could help in identifying abuse. However, ensuring the accuracy of these detections remains a significant challenge.

Severity Assessment

Child abuse is a sensitive issue requiring careful handling, especially when providing technological solutions. It's crucial to ensure the severity levels of detection are accurate and to send SOS alerts only when necessary. False alerts could cause unnecessary panic for guardians and provoke authorities.

Croudsourcing

Authorities may not always be available for swift help. Trained volunteers could offer timely assistance to children in need. Effective methodologies should be implemented to ensure multiple volunteers can coordinate and respond to incidents as quickly as possible.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to identify child abuse laveraging IoT and Artifical Intelligence?

Child sexual abuse has become a pressing issue worldwide. Despite the availability of various solutions, many have significant limitations. In the era of IoT and Artificial Intelligence, the challenge is to evaluate how these technologies can be leveraged to build a solution for the early detection and prevention of child sexual abuse.


Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution aims to address the critical issue of child sexual abuse by leveraging the power of Internet of Things (IoT) technology and artificial intelligence (AI). The system is designed to autonomously detect potential abuse instances and initiate appropriate responses without relying on manual activation, which may not be feasible in urgent situations. It processes audio feeds through two parallel pipelines: emotion classification and sentiment analysis. The emotion classification pipeline extracts Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCCs) from the audio feed and feeds them into a deep learning model to classify the child's emotions. In the sentiment analysis pipeline, an Automatic Speech Recognition system powered by the Wav2Vec model converts the audio into text, which is then processed using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques. TF-IDF vectors derived from the text are analyzed using an SVM-based model to detect profanity and assign weights to predictions.

The system aggregates data over 30-second intervals to calculate a severity score, which determines the need for sending SOS alerts to authorities and guardians via Firebase Cloud Messaging API. The alerts are disseminated through a mobile application for guardians and an administrator dashboard for child protection authorities.

Volunteers can interact with the system through a React Native frontend and Node.js backend. They can report potential abuse incidents, which are processed using the Haversine Formula to identify nearby volunteers and children. Machine learning models and computer vision algorithms assess the severity of reported incidents. The system alerts volunteers and parents based on the severity level, using IoT devices to further assess threat levels in low to medium severity cases.

The core IoT device, embedded in a child's shoe, includes sensors such as a gyroscope, accelerometer, microphone, and GPS, managed by a Raspberry Pi. These sensors collect data on the child's movements, environment, and possible danger signs, which are processed in real-time on a Flask server. A mobile application enables parents to set safe zones using Google Maps, with alerts triggered if the child leaves these zones or exhibits suspicious foot movements or sounds.

The foot motion analysis component uses accelerometers and gyroscopes to capture foot movements, which are classified using a Support Vector Classifier (SVC). The system detects patterns indicative of potential abuse, assigns weights to these movements, and aggregates the data to assess the likelihood of abuse.

This comprehensive approach, integrating IoT devices, real-time data processing, and advanced algorithms, aims to proactively intervene in potential abuse situations, safeguarding children and providing peace of mind for families.

Research Objectives

Device Development & Geofencing

The primary objective is to develop an IoT device integrated into a child’s shoe, equipped with sensors such as a gyroscope, accelerometer, microphone, and GPS, all powered by a Raspberry Pi. The device will continuously monitor the child's movements, environment, and location to identify potential danger signs. Additionally, the system aims to implement a robust geofencing feature that allows parents to define safe zones using a mobile application. Alerts will be triggered whenever the child moves outside these predefined areas, ensuring real-time protection and swift responses to potential threats.

Classification of Sexual Child Abuse Using Audio Analysis

The objective is to develop an advanced audio processing system for the detection of child sexual abuse. The system will utilize Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCCs) for emotion classification and the Wav2Vec model for converting speech to text, followed by Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to analyze the content for signs of abuse. The goal is to achieve high accuracy in identifying emotional distress and abusive language, which can then trigger immediate alerts to caregivers and authorities, enabling timely intervention in potential abuse situations.

Classification of Sexual Child Abuse Using Foot Motion Analysis

The objective is to design a motion detection system embedded in a child’s shoe to monitor foot movements and identify patterns indicative of potential abuse. Using accelerometers and gyroscopes, the system captures and analyzes detailed foot motion data. A Support Vector Classifier (SVC) will be employed to classify these movements, distinguishing between normal and suspicious activities. The goal is to develop a reliable method to detect abuse-related foot movements, providing an additional layer of automatic monitoring and protection for children.

Volunteer Involvement and Reporting

The objective is to engage volunteers in the system to enhance community-based monitoring and response to potential child abuse incidents. A mobile application will be developed to allow volunteers to report suspicious activities and share geolocations. The system will process these reports using machine learning models and computer vision algorithms to assess the severity of potential abuse incidents. By analyzing the proximity and combined reports from volunteers, the system aims to classify risk levels and notify nearby volunteers and parents accordingly. This collaborative approach aims to create a supportive network for rapid and effective intervention in child abuse cases.

Methodology

Figure: High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed solution comprises 7 main components:

  1. IoT Device with Integrated Sensors
  2. Geofencer
  3. Emotion Analyzer
  4. Sentimental Analyzer
  5. Foot Motion Analyzer
  6. Aggregator
  7. Volunteer Manager

The Wearable IoT device transmits audio feed, motion sensor data, and GPS data to the Flask Backend Server. The Sentimental Analyzer utilizes Wav2Vec to convert audio feeds into text and identifies spoken content related to sexual abuse. The Emotion Analyzer analyzes emotions using MFCC extracted from the audio feed. The Geofencer determines the child's location relative to a safezone using live GPS data. The Foot Motion Analyzer classifies foot motion related to child sexual abuse using gyroscope and accelerometer data.

Each module's results are forwarded to the Aggregator, which assigns weights to aggregate them over a 30-second period. The mean weighted result is calculated as the abuse score. A score greater than 7.5 indicates a high severity alert, 5 to 7.5 signifies moderate severity, and 3 to 5 denotes low severity; other values are ignored. Upon reaching a critical score, SOS notifications are sent to guardians and authorities.

Additionally, the Volunteer Manager, operating on a Node.js server, communicates with the Flask server to access real-time severity levels of children vulnerable to potential abuse. This data aids volunteers in identifying and assisting children in urgent need.
